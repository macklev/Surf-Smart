# Surf-Smart

Surf Smart is a application made by Mackenzie Levitt and Shannon Tuohey for the Spring 2026 SUNY New Paltz Hackathon. The purpose of Surf Smart is to address the issue of youth safety online. In a world where there is increasing internet usage for children, it is essential to ensure that kids are aware of the potential threats and know how to surf smart online. 

We chose this topic because as college students, we grew up in the mid-2000s where internet access was on the rise and threats were coming to light. Now, cyber threats are more prominent then ever, and children are less informed about what to do in situations and oftentimes fall into predator's traps. 

We began researching this issue by sending out a survey regarding internet usage, primarily to education majors. The data we obtained from the survey enlightened us on how major this issue really is. 

![Surf Smart Survey](./Images/SurfSmartSurvey.pdf)

As you can see, most respondants stated that children's internet usuage is fairly high, while their education in internet safety is quite low. When asked what age groups would benefit the most from internet safety education, 8-10, 10-12, and 6-8 were the most reccommended. 

Due to this data, we decided to move forward with this idea. To address the issue of internet safety, we targeted Surf Smart to young children within the age groups listed above. We coded 3 different games to test users's cyber safety, with a calming and kid-friendly interface.

Our project is different from other preexisting solutions because of its focus to the actual children that are effected by threats, and not parents or guardians. Surf Smart is primarily a children's website, although we do also feature other resources for parents to explore to help educate their kids further. 


What evidence is there to prove that your project can be effective?


Shannon focused on using HTML and CSS to create a visually pleasing and functional frontend, while Mackenzie used JavaScript to create the games. Shannon also designed the background of the website in Figma.
